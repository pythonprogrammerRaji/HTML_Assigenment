1.0 INTRODUCTION :

In the previous semester,we studied an algebraic system known as a group,equipped with one binary operation. In this chapter we shall study an important type of algebraic structure equipped with two binary operation known as a ring. The motivation arises from the fact that integers follow a definite pattern with respect to the ordinary addition and multiplication. We shall give an axiomatic definition of a ring and study some of its properties

1.1 RINGS

Definition :

An algebraic structure (R,+,*) consisting of a non-empty set R and two binary operations called addition and multiplication denoted by '+' and '*' is called following postulated are satisfied :

I

Addition is an abelian group with respect to addition. i.e (R,+) is an abelian group

i)Addition is associative i.e a+(b+c)=(a+b)+c, for all a,b,c belongs to R.

ii)There exists an element denoted by o in R such that a+o=a=o+a for all a belongs to R. The symbol 'o' denote the identity.

iii)For each a belongs to R, there exists -a belongs to R, such that a+(-a)=o=(=a)+a the symbol -a is the inverse of a.

iv)a+b=b+a, for all a,b belongs to R.

II

R is a semigroup for '.', i.e a.(b.c)=(a.b).c for all a,b,c belongs to R (multiplication is associative)

III

multiplication is distributive with respect to addition i.e for all a,b belongs to R

a.(b+c)=a.b+a.c Left distributive law

(b+c).a=b.a+c.a Right distributive law

Note

1. By saying that '+' is a binary operation in R, we mean for all a,b belongs to R, a+b belongs to R. i.e R is closed w.r.t addition. Similarly R is closed w,r,t multiplication for all a,b belongs to R, a.b belongs to R.

2. We shall use the symbol (R,+,.) to denote a ring structure.

3. The equation a+x=b has a unique solution b+(-a) in R and we simply write b-a and call the same the difference of b and a

4. Both the cancellation laws will hold good for addition in R. i.e for all a.b.c in R a+b = a+c == b=c amd b+a = c+a == b=c

Some special types of Rings

1) commutative Ring :

A ring (R,+,.) is called a commutative ring, if the binary composition multiplication is commutative, i.e if we have a.b = b.a for all a.b belongs to R.
